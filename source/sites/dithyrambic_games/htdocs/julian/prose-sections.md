
# Portfolio prose

## Bio

Hi, my name is Julian and I'm a game programmer from Berkeley, California. I just recently graduated from UC Berkeley where I studied Computer Science. I studied computer science because I've wanted to 
make video games since I was 13 years old and coding was my preferred way to create. My purpose in life is to create meaningful games that bring joy to the lives of other people. I began my six-year academic journey to meet that end and now that I've graduated and started to get more real game-making experience under my belt I can say it was well worth the effort!

I work mostly with Godot Engine (GDScript/C++) and Unity (C#), but I've also done a project in Unreal Engine 5 using Blueprints. I'm always eager to learn new things and I strive to understand as much as I can about each part of the entire stack from high level software down to low level hardware. My favorite programming language is actually C++! I hope to one day return to programming in C++ more frequently and working closer to the hardware--WowCube, Playdate and Raspberry Pi, I'm looking at you! 

I enjoy being a generalist programmer, I love to work on systems, shaders, user interface, physics, AI, optimization, simulation, business logic, gameplay, networking, tools, audio and everything else required to make a game. Over the course of two years working on my own game Zombulacrum in Godot 4 I've learned a lot by doing it all myself. While I can't say that I'm an expert in any one field, I know enough about each component of the game software stack to be able to navigate implementing whatever. And I'm always looking to improve my craft and grow as a programmer. 

Outside of being a programmer, I also consider myself and amateur visual artists and fledgling music-maker. I've been a doodler and an impromptu musician since I was a toddler and while I haven't spent as much time honing those crafts as I have with programming, I hope to one day have enough time away from the computer to pursue those forms of expression. Still, I actually do author my own art assets sometimes using digital drawing tablets and record my own sound effects using foley and synths. 

On the topic of human growth and expression I want to put my two cents on AI right here. I believe that AI is here to stay but I don't believe in letting the narratives of society skew my perception of the tools. I believe the AI tools and agents are very interesting from a computer scientist's perspective, very useful from an entrepreneur's perspective, and deeply troubling from an artist and craftsperson's perspective. I'm learning these tools like everyone else but I'm approaching with a healthy level of skepticism about the effects on human health and wellbeing. In a nutshell, I'm using them but I wish I didn't have to. And further, I'm very selective about how and when I use it. Coding is somewhat acceptable to me--if not less satisfying compared to hand-coding it all--but using it for artwork directly is where I start to feel icky. Anyways, rant over.

## Projects

### DreadReign

*DreadReign* is the most recent project that I've had the pleasure to work on. I joined the Mechanical Moonworks team late in development, inheriting a 10+ year old codebase as the main staff programmer. We are in the last year of development and I'm working closely with our lead designer and our lead art director on various systems to see this project through to completion. The game is written in C# in the Unity engine with PlasticSCM (now called Unity Version Control) as the versioning software. 

I'm working part time on this project so I still need to work (haha) so don't feel that this in any way disqualifies me from working on other projects! Currently I'm focused on implementing user interfaces and RPG systems. So far I've contributed by debugging, creating a map-style level selection menu, and helped scaffold the design for the RPG systems. 

The game is an ambitious project inspired by the likes of Gantlet and Doom. It seeks to combine the party based RPG elements of Gauntlet with the fast paced and immersive gameplay of Doom. Keep an eye out for the game's release by Q2 of 2027!


### helo

*helo* is the other main project I'm working on at the moment. 

Old Prose:
helo is a game I'm collaborating on with my good friend Josh. Born from a love of games where we would fly helicopters together in tightly knit co-op gameplay--like Battlefield 3--we are making a game that's also inspired by Kingdom: 2 Crowns with our own take on it. You fly a helicopter around, recruit mercenary troops, assign troop roles, build out your base, vie for resources and expand your private miliary company's operational territory. The game is meant to be lighthearted co-op fun that also points out and constrasts to the grim reality of PMCs in the third-world. Look out for our game releasing Steam sometime this year!

New prose:
The game is still is in development and rough around the edges but it's coming along really nicely and has a lot of fun elements already. It's been a blast to work on this over time with my good friend. We've found lots of interesting avenues of game design to explore and the idea has mutated a lot since the first seed of its inception. 

My contributions have been through coding, systems design and game design decisions. I've also done some audio work supplying sound effects and driving the music and sound effects via code. Something that I'm proud of on this project is how I helped to pioneer our unusual art style that is based around 3D rendered pixel sprites. It's a work in progress but the creativity is absolutely through the roof on this project! I'm also especially proud of how Josh and I have been able to riff on eachothers ideas and communicate and collaborate effectively with minimal friction.
